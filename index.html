70s Rock Music

Electric Light Orchestra

Formed in 1970 by Roy Wood and Jeff Lynne, their vision in creating ELO was to "pick up where the Beatles left off" by combining the sounds of rock with the elegance of classical music.

Total Album Sales: >50 million

Pink Floyd

Before leading the way in the progressive rock subgenre with the release of the Dark Side of the Moon, Pink Floyd were a middling psychedelic rock band with the potential to go anywhere.

Total Album Sales: >250 million

Led Zeppelin

Originally touted to sink like a "lead balloon" by almost-members Keith Moon and John Entwistle, Led Zeppelin did just the opposite and paved the way for hard rock and heavy metal.

Total Album Sales: >300 million

After a decade of laying the groundwork for rock music in the 60s, with much of the foundation laid by bands such as the Beatles, the Rolling Stones, the Jimi Hendrix Experience, the Doors, Cream and the Who, the 70s era of rock music was just nicely lined up for a new generation of rock n' rollers to push the boundaries of rock music. Besides the trio of bands featured here, there are of course many others who helped to shape the 70s rock scene and honourable mentions go out to other frontrunners such as Fleetwood Mac, Queen, Bruce Springsteen, Billy Joel and David Bowie, to name a few.
