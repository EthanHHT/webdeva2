Pink Floyd

The Dark Side of the Moon

Considered to be the best album of all time, but not the best Pink Floyd one, The Dark Side of the Moon was the peak of the progressive rock movement. A concept album, it touched on topics such as anxiety, time, death, greed, war and mental illness and no album since has resonated with the world as much it did.

Most Popular Track: Money

Released: 1973

Wish You Were Here

Dedicated to former band member and crazy diamond Syd Barrett, WYWH was recorded by an emotionally drained Pink Floyd wanting to mourn the loss of their former friend to drugs as well as hit back at the brutality of the machine that was the music industry.

Most Popular Track: Wish You Were Here

Released: 1975

The Wall

A rock opera about Pink, a former rock star who forms a figurative wall around himself built by him traumatic experiences, the Wall explored deeper into the human psyche, especially on topics such as abandonment, abuse and isolation.

Most Popular Track: Another Brick in the Wall, Pt. 2

Released: 1979

After the mental breakdown of their former frontman Syd Barrett, the quartet of Roger Waters, David Gilmour, Richard Wright and Nick Mason were stuck in a limbo for quite a few years. Did they want to carry on with their psychedelic identity pushed onto them by the public, or would they look to innovate and evolve further? 1970's Atom Heart Mother and 1971's Meddle, which included the 23 minute-long Echoes, proved they chose to head in the latter direction and 1973's The Dark Side of the Moon would prove to be their magnum opus of their prog-rock journey.
