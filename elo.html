Electric Light Orchestra

Out of the Blue

A concept album about the weather, lead singer Jeff Lynne conceptualised and wrote all the music for the douple LP within three and a half weeks after a sudden burst of creativity during a retreat at his rented chalet in the Swiss Alps.

Most Popular Track: Mr Blue Sky

Released: 1977

Discovery

"Disco? Very." To simply label Discovery as a disco album would be an insult to what is a great album filled with great love songs. Even with the success of Out of the Blue, Discovery is considered by most fans to be ELO's greatest album.

Most Popular Track: Don't Bring Me Down

Released: 1979

Time

Considered to be the first ever concept album about time travel, Time has divided fan opinion since its release given how much more "electronic" it sounds compared to the more orchestral style of previous albums.

Most Popular Track: Hold On Tight

Released: 1981

After spending their formative years having only relatively average success with albums such as On the Third Day, Eldorado and Face the Music, it was only after the release of Out of the Blue then did ELO start to achieve global fame as its extensive use of synthesizers and the Vocoder captured the attention and imagination of the masses. Following it up was the just as renowned Discovery and a string of subsequent hits and misses which include Time, the soundtrack to Xanadu, Secret Messages and Balance of Power.
