Led Zeppelin

IV

With no official title, Led Zeppelin's fourth studio album is commonly referred to IV, or Led Zeppelin IV. Recorded with criticism of Led Zeppelin III in mind, IV, with its high quality and impactful music, was the perfect answer to critics who still had any reservations about the band.

Most Popular Track: Stairway to Heaven

Released: 1971

Houses of the Holy

Following up an album as great as IV was going to be no easy feat, even for Led Zeppelin. But with Houses of the Holy, they did just that, abandoning their darker rock sound for a far more clean and refined style.

Most Popular Track: The Rain Song

Released: 1973

Physical Graffiti

A favourite, especially among die-hard fans, Physical Graffiti was the only double LP ever recorded by Led Zeppelin. Originally meant to be a single LP, an excess of material, especially good material, led to them deciding to push a double album instead.

Most Popular Track: Kashmir

Released: 1975

Originally supposed to be a supergroup fronted by guitarist Jimmy Page, joined by Jeff Beck of the Yardbirds as well as the Who's John Entwistle and Keith Moon, Jimmy Page eventually had to settle with forming a band with the likes of John Paul Jones, Robert Plant and John Bonham. From those early days in 1968 to John Bonham's untimely demise in 1980, Led Zeppelin would spend most of the 1970s leading the way in rock n' roll.
